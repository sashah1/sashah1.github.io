Should Skullcandy introduce true wireless headphones?

Project Details

Overview: Determine if Skullcandy should expand its product line to include a true wireless earbud, and determine which features their customer base would prefer.

Team:4 Engineers1 MBA

Research Methods:  Survey Design and Analysis
  
Tools: Qualtrics, SPSS, Excel

Timeline: September 2018 – December 2018 In the Fall of 2018, I took Marketing Research Design and Analysis, a course designed to teach students how to manage the marketing research process and provide information as an input to marketing decision-making. The course was all about data, and being an engineer, quantitative data analysis is my thing. For our final project we ran a large scale conjoint survey to determine if Skullcandy should expand its product line. The team used SPSS and Excel for the analysis, using crosstabs, conjoint, factor analysis and cluster analysis to make our final decision. Each type of analysis gave us different insights into Skullcandy’s user base, and in the end, we found that they should not pursue a high end, true wireless earbud as it doesn’t fit with their target audience.

Key Learnings: Research design and implementation, usage of commercial data sources, qualitative research (attitude measurement), survey methods, and the burgeoning uses of internet and web-based data.

Problem Definition

Skullcandy is looking to expand its product line to include a true wireless earbud, and is trying to determine whether this line extension makes sense. If so, it wants to determine what features its customer base would like in a product placed at a higher price point than its existing earphone products. It should be in line with its current target demographic and market space, primarily action sports, and the general consumer market, as well as maintaining their brand of rebel punk.

Research questions to support our decision problem:
1. How many people use the headphones that come with their phone?
2. How long do people keep their headphones?
3. Do people prefer wired, wireless, or true wireless headphones?
4. How important is sound quality? (clarity and level of treble and bass in headphones)
5. Do people want fitness features? (heart rate, temperature, GPS, etc)
6. How important is water protection?
7. Does the shape of the earbud affect the decision to buy a pair of headphones?
8. Is there a demand for smart features? (i.e. Alexa compatibility)
9. How much are people willing to pay for those features?

Preliminary Research

The global headphone market is expected to grow at a CAGR of ~7% from 2018-2023. As one of the top five headphone brands, Skullcandy is poised to increase its profits in the expanding market. With the earbud segment expected to grow at a CAGR of 30%, Skullcandy can best capitalize by offering a new product in the sector.
The 4 Ps for Marketing:
We utilized the 4 Ps of marketing to get a better understand of the product and how it’s distributed. 

Product:
We define earphones as speaker units that sit within the ear and headphones as speakers with cups that sit on or over the ear, connected by a headband. At the current state, a standard feature across all styles is a microphone and buttons for pause and play actions. Additional actions of the buttons include answer or hang up phone calls, move to next song, or move to previous song. Smart features include voice control and fitness features. The latter is a subjective measure, however it is often judged as one of three common sound profiles among bass-heavy, v-shaped, and flat. Aside from sound, which is a feature in all headphones, a common premium feature is noise canceling, which can be active or passive.

Promotions:
Skullcandy offers three promotions:
     1. Buy two, get 20% off for Smokin Buds 2 and Strum earphone products
     2. General sale discounts ranging from 30% to 55% for different products
     3. Free Android phone with purchase provided the customer pay $50 for the first month of service and enroll in autopay.

Price:


Placement:
Skullcandy offers products through both online and traditional retail outlets. These include general goods stores, like Amazon or Target, electronic retailers like Best Buy, and outdoor or sporting retailers like REI.

3C’s Gut Check (Company, Customer, Competitors)
We used the 3C’s model to gut check our findings and assumptions. We found that…
     • Skullcandy’s target customers generally include cheaper younger people, people who want a colorful headphones, and - more generally - the skateboarder, surfer, rebel demographic
     • Its primary target market may be fruitful, as many skateboarders and surfers live active lifestyles and could use a flexible alternative to Skullcandy’s current set of earbuds
     • If Skullcandy makes an affordable product that can withstand swift movements without tangled wires getting in the way, this demographic would likely be receptive to trying it
     • Many of Skullcandy’s competitors make true wireless headphones typically priced over $100.
     • Skullcandy competes on price and style with other headphone brands and could do so in this category, especially given the high price point for most true wireless earbuds and the fact that many Skullcandy users do not yet own true wireless earbuds 

Data Analysis

Methods

Conjoint
We used SPSS to build conjoint variables, and ran the survey using Qualtrics. We created a baseline of wired earphones with no features at $19.99. This left six variables among the four categories: Connection type (wireless or true wireless), Earfit (earbud), Health Features Via App (yes), and Price ($99.99, or 199.99). Then the partworths for each of the variables were found and those were used to determine people's willingness to pay for each feature.

Crosstabs
The crosstabs and T-test allow Skullcandy to determine which combination of variables show significance allowing them to target specific groups for specific products. We performed these tests on 14 combinations of variables from our survey. These combinations based off the following variables: Income, brand, ownership time, gender, time listened to per day, age, connection style, and earfit. To run the tests we used a One-way Anova or Chi-Square for each of the combination depending on whether they were a continuous or discrete variable.

Factor Analysis
Factor analysis was performed on two sets of independent variables: first psychographics and lifestyle, then importance of various features. The factors were then used to in a regression of willingness to pay from the conjoint analysis.

Cluster Analysis
A cluster analysis was performed on the four categories from our conjoint survey. This was performed twice, first creating three clusters then with four clusters. The cluster analysis utilized the K-Means technique to iterate and find clusters.

Key Findings

Our raw data produced some interesting key findings that helped guide our recommendations.
     • 60.5% of our respondents use the headphones that come with their phone
     • 37.2% of respondents keep their headphones between 6 months and a year
     • 39.5% prefer having a headset that forms a seal in their ear canal
     • Sound quality is the most important attribute when buying headphones followed by price
     • People are willing to pay more for wireless headphones over true wireless
     • Wired headphones were the least preferable

Our factor and cluster analysis validated that respondents answered consistently to the lifestyleand conjoint questions, as the lifestyle factor analysis and the cluster analysis found the same number of factors.We created three personas:
     1. “Routine-Based Homebody” who cares about health features and not about sound Quality
     2. “Media Pioneer” who is passionate about wireless, true wireless, sound quality and is willing to pay more for better headphones, is always on the hunt for the next new thing
     3. “Traditional Fitness” who is not worried about most quality features except for earfit, as they want comfort when they are moving

Recommendations

We flatly recommend that Skullcandy does not pursue a true wireless headphone at this time. As a company, Skullcandy has positioned itself as a budget-friendly, affordable brand, marketed towards the often low-income outdoor action sports enthusiasts. These customers are not likely to be able to afford a more expensive set of true wireless headphones.

Limitations to Research

Our primary limitation was a lack of demographic diversity. Most of the respondents to oursurvey were Marketing 618 students. As a result, nearly every respondent had the same education level, approximate age, and income. Our team at times had trouble in finding significant results in our survey data - especially when looking at demographic data - and lack of diversity may have been a contributing factor.

Many headphone purchasers make their decisions based on packaging, fit, and overall aesthetics. These traits are mostly visual and are difficult to describe in a survey without letting the potential customer see them in person. While we were able to gather information about other desired qualities in headphones, design is a vital aspect that was difficult to glean from a written survey. We therefore would advise Skullcandy to supplement this survey with focus groups among its target customers in order to better understand their desired feel and look of headphones.
